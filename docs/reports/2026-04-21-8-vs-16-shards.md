# 8-vs-16 shards: HC backfill A/B

**Date:** 2026-04-21.
**Context:** [`docs/current_progress.md § Experiment — 8 vs 16 shards`](../current_progress.md).
**Archive pointers:**
- Arm A full snapshot: [`docs/progress_archive/2026-04-21_0805_hc-2025-arm-a-8-shard-cliff-cascade.md`](../progress_archive/2026-04-21_0805_hc-2025-arm-a-8-shard-cliff-cascade.md)
- Arm A run dir: `runs/active/2026-04-20-hc-2025/`
- Arm B run dir: `runs/active/2026-04-21-hc-2024/`

## Executive summary

16 shards beats 8 shards decisively. **Arm B completed ~92% of a
workload 5% larger than arm A's in ~30 min wall-clock vs arm A's
3h 03m productive (and never finishing)** — 6× faster to comparable
coverage with 3 cliffs instead of 8. The dominant drivers are (i)
halved per-shard workload keeping shards inside the L2-engagement
horizon (H4 confirmed), and (ii) freshly-fetched proxies eliminating
tenacity retry-chains (H6, not originally hypothesized, emerged as
the single largest per-request speedup factor). Sticky duration
10→5 likely contributed modestly (H5, not cleanly isolated).

**Decision:** use **16 shards + fresh proxies + sticky=5** as the
default config for all future year-backfill sweeps (arms C and
onward). 8-shard configuration is retired for big-workload jobs.

## Cliff analysis

**Arm A (8/8 cliffed across 2.5h window)** — classic L2+ adaptive-
block cascade. First cliff at 55 min (shard-h); last cliff at ~3h05m
(shard-a). Cliff ordering correlated with each pool's residual L3
reputation from the prior day's 2026 sweep — direct evidence of
per-exit-IP reputation decay across days (not fully cleared by
overnight idle).

**Arm B (3/16 cliffed):**
- **shard-o (2.2%, record 20)** — **detector false positive**, not a
  WAF signal. One 66.67s HTTP record at the MIN_OBS=20 boundary where
  `int(0.95 * 20) = 19` made p95 equal to the max. Fix (axis-B
  window-full gate) landed mid-arm-B under `judex/sweeps/shared.py`;
  running shards used the old detector. Lost 879 pids.
  [Commit: 8b… (see progress doc § What just landed).]
- **shard-b (81.2%, record 731)** — genuine late-stage L2+ cliff.
  Shard had accumulated `warn=50` regime observations by this point.
- **shard-n (89.0%, record 800)** — genuine late-stage L2+ cliff.
  Similar pattern to shard-b.

**Honest cliff count: 2 genuine + 1 detector-FP = 3.** H4 predicted
≤3 cliffs on the headroom-budget model. **H4 confirmed.** (With the
window-full fix applied prospectively, future runs would count 2.)

## Hypothesis outcomes

### H4 — Pool-headroom-vs-workload budget model (CONFIRMED)

Per-shard workload_time must fit under effective_budget
≈ N_proxies × T_L2 − residual_L3_debt.

- Arm A: 1,720 pids / 0.15 rps ≈ 3h10m workload vs. 0.5–3h debt-
  adjusted budget → **cliff scheduled** (observed 8/8, matches).
- Arm B: 899 pids / 0.66 rps ≈ 23 min workload vs. ~2–3h budget →
  **comfortable margin, 0–3 cliffs predicted** (observed 2 genuine,
  matches the ≤3 bound).

**Operational heuristic going forward:** size per-shard slice so
`workload_time ≤ 0.3 × effective_budget` to keep expected cliff
count near zero. For freshly-fetched pools, that's roughly "keep
each shard's slice under ~800 pids."

### H5 — Sticky 10→5 (UNDETERMINED)

Not cleanly isolated from H6 in arm B. Possible signal: two
arm-B cliffs (b, n) happened at ~80–89% completion, suggesting
L3 debt began mattering near shard's end — consistent with
sticky=5's predicted "spread L3 across more IPs" effect buying
extra headroom early but not late. **Arm C (planned: sticky=5)
doesn't isolate this either.** A targeted follow-up (arm-A
recovery split sticky=5 vs sticky=10 on fresh IPs at the same
time of day) would.

### H6 — Proxy freshness dominates throughput (STRONGLY SUPPORTED)

The 4.4× per-shard throughput gain (0.15 → 0.66 rec/s) traces
almost entirely to tenacity retry-403 chains not firing on fresh
IPs. Driver-log comparison:
- Arm A driver log: records `ok` with walls 5–13s (tenacity
  absorbed 403s, averaged backoff into the wall).
- Arm B driver log: records `ok` with walls 0.5–1.5s (one HTTP
  call per record, no retries).

STF's L1 per-IP throttle fires at ~80–100 req per 5-min window.
Arm A's IPs were at or near threshold from prior days' sweeps;
every few records they'd nudge into 403 territory. Arm B's IPs
were effectively at zero, absorbing the full workload below the
threshold for the duration of the sweep.

**Ops consequence (landed as discipline in `docs/current_progress.md
§ Request-footprint reduction`):** refresh proxies before every
sustained sweep. It's the single highest-leverage knob.

## Decision rule outcome

Decision rule from the progress doc:
- **16 wins** → wall-clock_B < 0.65 × wall-clock_A AND cliff_B ≤ cliff_A + 1.
- **16 loses** → wall-clock_B > 0.85 × wall-clock_A OR cliff_B ≥ 2 × cliff_A.

Observed: wall-clock ratio = 0.17× (way better than 0.65× bar), cliff
count 3 vs 8 (well below 2×). **16 wins decisively.**

## Decisions

1. **Arm C (HC 2023) runs at 16 shards + fresh proxies + sticky=5** —
   same config as arm B. 12,644-pid workload at 16 shards = 790 pids/
   shard, smaller than arm B's 899. Expect completion in ~25 min with
   0–2 cliffs.
2. **Arm-A recovery** (6,399 pids from 2025's cliffed 8-shard run) +
   arm-B recovery (~1,147 pids from 2024's 3 cliffed shards). Combine
   into one recovery CSV, run direct-IP single-thread with
   `--no-stop-on-collapse`. Non-urgent; run after arm C.
3. **8-shard config is retired** for year-backfill workloads. Not
   deleted — remains in the CLI for small/ad-hoc sweeps — just not
   the default for sustained jobs.
4. **Proxy refresh is now a preflight step** for any sustained
   sweep. Document as a one-line entry in the sweep launch
   checklist.

## Caveats / things this doesn't settle

- **H5 is open.** Sticky duration's contribution couldn't be isolated
  from proxy freshness. The cleanest future test: on the combined
  recovery CSV, shard half at sticky=5 and half at sticky=10, same
  pool batch, same time of day.
- **Time-of-day effect is unmeasured.** Arm A ran evening BRT, arm B
  ran morning BRT. Morning might be the WAF's most permissive posture
  independent of everything else. To isolate: rerun arm-A recovery
  twice at different times.
- **ASN-level WAF behavior not tested at 32+ shards.** All arm-B
  throughput was through scrapegw's ASN at ~63 STF req/sec. At 32
  shards we'd hit ~125 req/sec, which may cross a different WAF
  threshold. Backlog § Request-footprint reduction items should land
  before attempting 32 shards; they reduce per-case STF calls 30–50%
  and give us a politeness cushion.
- **The CliffDetector fix is prospective.** Arm B's 1 FP would have
  been ~0 FP under the new detector, but arm B itself used the old
  one throughout. Future arms benefit.
